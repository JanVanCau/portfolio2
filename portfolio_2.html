Predictive time series model on Naming Trends in Belgium

Check out code on GitHub

I downloaded the csv files on first names from the website of Statbel, the Belgian statistical office. They contain all first names of newborns with a frequency of 5 or more, with tables for each year from 1995 up to and including 2023. There are separate files for boys and girls.

Upon performing data cleaning and organization on the year tables, I merged them into one, with a column for each year. I then constructed a time series forecasting model for each name. I used polynomials and Fourier terms on the time steps to model the long-term trend and seasonality. For short term cycles and variability I added lag terms. I added XGBoost to model the error terms, but noticed that for some names it led to overfitting.

I used the 1995-2020 period as training data and the 2021-2023 period as validation data. I performed a grid search to find, for each separate name, the optimal polynomial degree, number of Fourier terms, number of lag terms and whether or not to model the error terms. I achieved a good fit, with a median RMSE of 2.858 for girls' names and 3.367 for boys' names.

According to the model, there will be some interesting evolutions in the Top 5 in 2024! Olivia will stay on top in the girls' names, but Alba will gain a lot in popularity and achieve 2nd place. In the boys' category, a new winner will emerge: Adam will rise from position 4 to position 1. Louis will drop out of the Top 5, to be replaced by Tiago.

Kaggle competition: Binary Prediction with a Rainfall Dataset

Check out code on Kaggle

The goal of this competition was to predict rainfall based on meteorological parameters such as temperature, dewpoint, pressure and windspeed. Submissions were evaluated on area under ROC curve. My model achieved a score of 0.89890 on the test data, which placed me at no. 438 out of 4381 submissions.

My first step consisted of exploratory data analysis (EDA), which showed the importance of the humidity, cloud and sunshine features. I then explored multiple machine learning models, applying the Optuna framework on each of them for hyperparameter optimization. Finally, I constructed a voting model to act as an ensemble learner, combining the predictions of the separate models.
